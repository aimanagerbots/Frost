# Frost Cannabis Brand Guidelines

> **The Single Rule**: Every piece of Frost copy should sound like a reliable friend at the dispensary who tells you what's actually good — no hype, no lecture, just straight talk.

---

## Voice Identity

Frost sounds like the friend who always has a solid recommendation. Not the guy who won't shut up about terpene profiles, and not the brand screaming "PREMIUM CRAFT" on every label. Frost is the neighbor who says "I grabbed this last week, it's good" and you trust them because they've never steered you wrong. Calm confidence. No try-hard energy. The product does the talking.

### Voice Characteristics

| Characteristic | What This Means | Example |
|----------------|-----------------|---------|
| **Honest** | Say what it is. No inflated claims, no marketing spin. | "20% THC. Smooth smoke. That's it." |
| **Casual** | Talk like a real person, not a brand. Contractions, short sentences. | "We're not fancy. We're consistent." |
| **Direct** | Get to the point. Respect their time and intelligence. | "Same strain. Same quality. Every time." |
| **Warm but not soft** | Friendly without being bubbly. Approachable without being a pushover. | "You'll be back. They always come back." |

### We Are / We Are NOT

| We Are... | We Are NOT... |
|-----------|---------------|
| The brand you trust | The brand you flex |
| Straightforward | Salesy |
| Affordable and good | Cheap or cutting corners |
| Consistent | Boring |
| Confident | Arrogant |
| Your go-to | Your experiment |

## Tone by Context

Voice is constant. Tone flexes based on context.

| Context | Tone Shift | Example |
|---------|------------|---------|
| **Packaging** | Minimal, confident, let the product stand | "Frost Blue Dream. Indica Hybrid. 22% THC." |
| **Dispensary Shelf** | Quick hook, stands out without shouting | "Good weed. Fair price. Every time." |
| **Website / Landing** | Slightly warmer, tell the brand story | "We started Frost because we got tired of paying too much for inconsistent weed." |
| **Social Media** | Most relaxed, community feel | "Friday pickup sorted. What Frost strain are you grabbing this weekend?" |
| **Product Descriptions** | Helpful, sensory, no jargon | "Smooth, mellow, and easy to come back to. Great for winding down after work." |
| **Error / Out of Stock** | Honest, no corporate apology | "Sold out. That happens with this one. Check back next week." |

## Language Rules

### USE These Words/Phrases

| Word/Phrase | When to Use | Instead of |
|-------------|-------------|------------|
| "Consistent" | Describing product quality | "Artisanal" or "handcrafted" |
| "Every time" | Reinforcing reliability | "Experience excellence" |
| "Solid" | Casual endorsement | "Premium" or "luxury" |
| "Smooth" | Describing smoke/flavor | "Notes of..." or "bouquet" |
| "Fair price" | Talking about cost | "Affordable luxury" or "value proposition" |
| "Real" | Describing the brand ethos | "Authentic" (overused) |
| "Go-to" | Brand positioning | "The ultimate choice" |

### AVOID These Words/Phrases

| Word/Phrase | Why | Use Instead |
|-------------|-----|-------------|
| "Premium" | Every brand says it. Means nothing. | "Quality" or just describe the product |
| "Craft" / "Artisanal" | Triggers the snobby vibe we're avoiding | "Properly grown" or "done right" |
| "Elevated experience" | Corporate cannabis cliche | "Good smoke" or "smooth hit" |
| "Curated" | Pretentious | "Selected" or just name the products |
| "Luxury" | Not our customer, not our brand | Don't reference price tier — just be good |
| "ACT NOW" / "LIMITED TIME" | Salesy hype, the opposite of Frost | Let the product create urgency naturally |
| "Cultivar" / "Terpene profile" | Jargon that alienates our customer | "Strain" and just describe how it tastes/feels |
| "Exquisite" / "Refined" | Sounds like wine, not weed | "Clean" or "smooth" |

### Product Terminology

| Term | Definition | Usage |
|------|------------|-------|
| Strain name | Always use the common name | "Blue Dream" not "Blue Dream #4 Phenotype B" |
| Type | Indica, Sativa, Hybrid | Always include on packaging, keep it simple |
| THC % | Lab-tested number | State it plainly. Don't inflate or hide it. |
| Product category | Flower, Pre-roll, Vape, Edible, Concentrate | Use everyday words. "Vape" not "cartridge delivery system." |

### Style Rules

- **Contractions**: Yes, always. "We're" not "We are." "You'll" not "You will."
- **Sentence length**: Short. 5-12 words is the sweet spot. Break up anything longer.
- **Paragraph length**: 2-3 sentences max. White space is your friend.
- **Emoji**: No. Not on packaging, not on the website, not in emails. Keep it clean.
- **Exclamation marks**: Rarely. One per page max. Frost is confident, not excited.
- **Oxford comma**: Yes.
- **Capitalization**: Sentence case everywhere. Title Case only for product names and strain names.

## Messaging Framework

### The Hook

> **Good weed shouldn't cost a fortune.**

### Core Value Propositions

**Value Prop 1: Consistent Quality**
- **The claim**: Same strain, same experience, every time.
- **Why it matters**: Your customer's #1 frustration is buying the same product and getting a different result. Frost eliminates that.
- **Proof point**: Same sourcing, same standards, same testing protocols across every batch.
- **Sample copy**: "You liked it last time. You'll like it this time."

**Value Prop 2: Fair Pricing**
- **The claim**: Quality cannabis at honest prices.
- **Why it matters**: Customers are sick of paying $60 an eighth for marketing and packaging. Frost puts the money in the product, not the label.
- **Proof point**: Compare our quality to brands charging 2x the price.
- **Sample copy**: "Good weed. Fair price. That's the whole pitch."

**Value Prop 3: No Hype, Just Product**
- **The claim**: What you see is what you get.
- **Why it matters**: Every brand claims to be "premium" and "craft." Customers can't tell what's real anymore. Frost cuts through by just being honest.
- **Proof point**: Real lab results, no inflated THC claims, straightforward product descriptions.
- **Sample copy**: "We don't do hype. We do consistent."

## Copy Patterns

### Headlines

- **Pattern**: Short statement. Period. Confidence, not shouting.
- **Length**: 3-7 words.
- **Good examples**:
  - "Same quality. Every time."
  - "Good weed shouldn't cost a fortune."
  - "Your new go-to."
  - "We don't do hype."
- **Bad examples**:
  - "Experience the Ultimate Premium Cannabis Journey!"
  - "Artisanally Crafted for the Discerning Connoisseur"
  - "SHOP NOW AND SAVE BIG!!!"

### Subheads

- **Pattern**: Expand on the headline with one clear thought.
- **Length**: 8-15 words.
- **Good examples**:
  - "Frost is the brand you come back to because it's always the same."
  - "We put our money in the product, not the packaging."
- **Bad examples**:
  - "Discover our carefully curated selection of artisanal cannabis products"
  - "Leveraging our expertise to deliver an unparalleled smoking experience"

### CTAs (Calls to Action)

- **Pattern**: Simple verb + object. No pressure.
- **Good examples**:
  - "Find Frost near you"
  - "See our strains"
  - "Check the menu"
- **Bad examples**:
  - "SHOP NOW!"
  - "Unlock Your Premium Experience"
  - "Don't Miss Out!"

### Product Descriptions

- **Tone**: Helpful, sensory, brief. Like a budtender giving a quick recommendation.
- **Pattern**: What it is + how it smokes/tastes + when to use it.
- **Good examples**:
  - "Blue Dream. Smooth, mellow, a little sweet. Good for winding down after work or a lazy weekend afternoon."
  - "Pineapple Express. Bright, energetic, a little citrus on the exhale. Grab this one before a hike or a hangout."
- **Bad examples**:
  - "Our Blue Dream cultivar features dominant myrcene terpenes with subtle notes of blueberry and earthy undertones."
  - "An exquisite sativa-dominant hybrid that elevates the senses."

### Error / Out of Stock

- **Tone**: Matter-of-fact, not apologetic. Brief.
- **Pattern**: What happened + when to check back.
- **Good examples**:
  - "Sold out. This one goes fast. Check back next week."
  - "Not available at this location. Try [other store]."
- **Bad examples**:
  - "We sincerely apologize for the inconvenience! This product is temporarily unavailable."
  - "Oopsie! Looks like we're all out!"

## Quick Reference

**Before writing, check**:
- [ ] Does this sound like a real person talking?
- [ ] Would a 28-year-old regular smoker relate to this?
- [ ] Am I using simple, everyday words?
- [ ] Is this the shortest way to say it?
- [ ] Did I avoid all the words on the AVOID list?
- [ ] Would this feel honest on the shelf next to a hype brand?

## Customer Context

> *Pulled from CUSTOMER.md for reference*

**Who we're talking to**: Adults 25-40 in Washington state. Working professionals, regular cannabis consumers. They know what they like and they're tired of being burned by inconsistent, overpriced products.

**Their main pain**: Overpriced hype brands that charge for packaging and marketing, not quality. Inconsistency between batches of the same product.

**What they value**: Consistent quality, fair pricing, honesty, clean product.

**What turns them off**: Snobby/pretentious branding, trend-chasing, brands that try too hard to be "premium" or "luxury."
